About · 01

Who We Are

We are a group of people who care deeply about learning—not as a concept, but as a lived experience. We have watched students spend hours studying, only to feel no closer to understanding. We have seen parents invest their savings in preparation, only to watch their children struggle on exam day. And we have asked ourselves: Why is this still so hard?

PrepHooks is our answer to that question. It is a tool designed to help students prepare for high-stakes exams through focused, intelligent practice. It pays attention to how you perform, shows you where you need to work, and guides your progress with honesty and clarity.

We are not educators who built a product. We are product people who built a learning system. That distinction shapes everything we do.

About · 02

Why We Started

We started PrepHooks because we noticed something troubling.

For decades, the way students prepare for exams has remained largely unchanged. The tools have evolved—from printed past questions to digital question banks—but the underlying approach has not. Students are still expected to sit with a repository of content and figure out, on their own, what to study, when to study it, and whether they are making progress.

Meanwhile, the world around them has changed. The way students engage with information, the way their attention is shaped by the devices in their pockets, the sheer volume of distraction they navigate daily—all of this has shifted. The preparation system has not moved with it.

We started PrepHooks because we believed there was a better way. Not a more complicated way. Not a more technological way. A more human way.

About · 03

What We Observed

The more we looked, the more we saw a pattern.

Students were not failing because they lacked access to content. Past questions have existed for decades. Textbooks are everywhere. Search engines and large language models have made finding answers almost effortless. Content was never the problem.

Students were not failing because they lacked effort. We met students who spent hours each day with their noses in books, who attended tutorial centers, who sacrificed sleep and social life—and still walked into the exam hall uncertain of their readiness.

The gap was not in what they had. The gap was in how they studied.

Many students default to methods that feel productive but are not: re-reading notes, highlighting text, cramming the night before. These approaches create the illusion of understanding—familiarity mistaken for knowledge.

Research has shown this pattern for decades. Yet it persists because studying effectively is not intuitive. It must be built into the experience. It must be made simple and obvious, so that students can focus on learning rather than on figuring out how to learn.

About · 04

What We Decided to Do

We decided to build a tool that would bridge that gap.

Not by adding more content. Not by dressing up the same old approach with flashy features. But by building a system that actually supports how learning happens.

We built a loop:

Teach — We provide clear, bite-sized lessons that build understanding without overwhelming the mind.

Test — We offer questions that target areas needing attention, based on how you have performed before.

Track — We show you a visual map of your progress, honest and unsparing, so you always know where you stand.

We made the experience feel like a conversation, not a transaction. One question at a time. One swipe at a time. No complicated dashboards. No decision fatigue. Just a clear path forward.

About · 05

What We Believe

We believe effective preparation should be straightforward and truthful.

We believe that students deserve to know where they actually stand—not where they wish they were, and not where a flattering score suggests they are. Honest feedback is the foundation of real improvement.

We believe that a learning tool should get out of the way. It should not compete for attention. It should not demand that students figure out how to use it. It should be simple, focused, and quiet—so that students can focus on the work that matters.

We believe that personalization is not a buzzword. It is a necessity. Every student is different. Their strengths are different. Their weaknesses are different. The pace at which they learn is different. A system that treats everyone the same is a system that helps no one as well as it could.
